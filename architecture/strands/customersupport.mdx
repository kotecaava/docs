## AI Support Layer ‚Äì What AI Handles

**Guiding question**

> _How does AI assist or handle support interactions?_

### Capabilities

- Instantly answer common questions using **knowledge articles + semantic search**.
- Draft responses for human agents to **approve or edit**.
- Summarize long threads or conversations before escalation.
- Predict **intent** and route tickets automatically.
- Identify duplicates and **merge similar issues**.
- Generate troubleshooting checklists based on user environment.

### AI Personality

- **Tone:** clear, polite, concise, neutral, professional.

**Rules**

- Never guess ‚Äî **defer to humans** when unsure.
- Be transparent about being an **AI assistant**.
- Avoid promises that depend on **engineering timelines**.
- Always provide **actionable next steps**.

### Responsible AI Practices

- AI never sees content the user **cannot access**.
- Sensitive data **masked** automatically in logs.
- Users can **escalate to human** at any moment.
- All AI interactions logged to improve **future accuracy**.

## Human Support Layer ‚Äì What Only Humans Do

**Guiding question**

> _What do human agents uniquely provide?_

### Core Responsibilities

- Empathy, emotional intelligence, reassurance, clarity.
- Judgment when issues are **ambiguous or technical**.
- Understanding of nuance, edge cases, and user history.
- Building **trust and long-term relationships**.
- Escalating **clearly documented issues** to product & engineering.

### Skills Required

- Strong written communication.
- Patient and respectful tone.
- Technical understanding of the product.
- Ability to **triage urgency**.
- Calm under pressure.
- Structured problem analysis.

### Playbook Structure

- Ticket intake checklist.
- Troubleshooting decision tree.
- Edge-case handling.
- AI suggestions review.
- Customer follow-up.
- Post-resolution summary.

## Knowledge System ‚Äì The Brain Behind Support

**Guiding question**

> _How does the support team keep knowledge up to date?_

### Components

- **Knowledge base**
  - Articles, guides, tutorials.
  - Single source of truth for **AI and human agents**.
- **Internal runbooks**
  - Step-by-step fixes for recurring issues:
    - auth problems,
    - integrations,
    - permissions,
    - environment quirks.
- **Tagging system**
  - Tag by **product area, severity, persona, device, integration**.
- **Feedback loop**
  - Agents mark articles as **inaccurate or outdated**.
  - Content team updates quickly.

### AI Integration

- AI auto-suggests **relevant articles**.
- AI flags outdated content based on **ticket patterns**.
- AI drafts article updates for **human review**.

<info>
  If support is constantly rewriting the same answer,\
  your knowledge system ‚Äî not your agents ‚Äî is failing.
</info>

## Tooling & Infrastructure ‚Äì The Support Stack

**Core Systems**

- **CRM / Ticketing platform**
  - Examples: Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk, Salesforce Service Cloud.
  - Central hub for **issues, SLAs, assignment, history**.
- **AI Copilot for Agents**
  - Built-in LLM assistant or 3rd-party tools.
  - Draft replies, summarize threads, classify tickets.
- **Knowledge base CMS**
  - Versioning, publishing workflows, content analytics.
- **Monitoring & Logging**
  - Examples: Sentry, Datadog, Grafana.
  - Links **platform health** with support escalations.
- **In-app support widget**
  - AI chat, surfaced articles, escalation triggers.

## üìà Escalation Paths ‚Äì How Serious Issues Move

**Guiding question**

> _How do unresolved or serious issues escalate?_

### Escalation Steps

- **Tier 0 ‚Üí Tier 1** if AI cannot confidently answer.
- **Tier 1 ‚Üí Tier 2** for technical analysis or reproduction.
- **Tier 2 ‚Üí Engineering** if likely a bug or backend issue.
- **Any tier ‚Üí Security** if suspicious activity is detected.
- **Enterprise ‚Üí CSM** if the relationship is at risk.

### Incident Response

- Support alerts **SRE or on-call engineer**.
- Issue logged in **incident tracker**.
- Customers updated during incident every **30‚Äì60 minutes**.
- Post-incident **RCA** shared with affected customers.

## üìä Metrics & Reporting ‚Äì How Support Is Measured

**Support Metrics**

- First response time.
- Full resolution time.
- AI resolution rate (Tier 0 success).
- Escalation rate (to Tier 2/3).
- Ticket backlog.
- Customer Satisfaction (**CSAT**).

**Experience Metrics**

- Effort score (how hard it was to get help).
- Reopened ticket rate.
- Time to first meaningful response (not just ‚Äúwe got your ticket‚Äù).
- Drop-off rate from self-serve to human channels.

## üö® Risk & Governance ‚Äì How Support Can Fail (and How You Prevent It)

**Key Risks**

- Over-reliance on AI causing **incorrect or insensitive answers**.
- Missing or slow escalations for **critical incidents**.
- Data leakage via support logs or screenshots.
- Inconsistent responses across agents and regions.
- Under-reporting of systemic issues to product and security.

**Guardrails**

- Clear policies on **when AI must hand off** to humans.
- Mandatory escalation rules for **security, billing, and data-loss issues**.
- Access controls and redaction for **sensitive data**.
- Standardized macros and templates for **high-risk scenarios**.
- Regular **QA reviews** and audits of support conversations.

## üß© How to Use This Customer Support Strand in Practice

1. **Map your current reality**
   - Export recent tickets.
   - Tag them across **channels, tiers, product areas**.
2. **Design your tiering + AI model**
   - Decide what belongs in **Tier 0 AI** vs **Tier 1‚Äì3 humans**.
   - Write explicit **handoff rules**.
3. **Codify your knowledge system**
   - Turn recurring answers into **articles and runbooks**.
   - Connect them to both **AI and humans**.
4. **Wire tooling + SLAs**
   - Align CRM, AI copilot, KB, and monitoring into **one Support OS**.
   - Make SLAs visible internally and in **customer commitments**.
5. **Close the loop**
   - Turn support data into **roadmap, UX, and doc changes** every cycle.
   - Measure **AI vs human** resolution impact over time.
